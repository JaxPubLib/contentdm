Jacksonville Public Library Digital Collections

African American History Digital Community

The African American History Digital Community Archive is a curated local history collection of original source material related to African American life in Northeast Florida. The collection includes photographs, personal papers, scrapbooks, yearbooks, pamphlets, and gray literature related to civic organizations or commercial enterprises. In the Digital Community Archive, the Library provides access to a collection of unique original source material from a variety of local resources, including community members and partner organizations, that would be otherwise unavailable to the general public.

Voices of the First Coast

Voices of the First Coast is a local initiative modeled after StoryCorps, a nationwide story project, designed to record, preserve and share the stories from our local community.

Loyd Sandgren Photographs Collection

Loyd Sandgren (1916-2001) was a Jacksonville-based commercial photographer who primarily worked from the 1940s through the 1990s. The Jacksonville Public Library acquired a collection of over 14,000 prints and thousands of negatives.

Old City Cemetery

Documents and records related to Old City Cemetery, the largest graveyard in Duval County that was established before 1880.

American Civil War Memorabilia

Items held by the Jacksonville Public Library Florida Collection related to the American Civil War.

Jacksonville FL Biographical Sketches

This city and its inhabitants have many stories to tell. How did we get here? Where are we going? 

Jacksonville Public Library System

Photographs, slides and video of Jacksonville Public Library buildings, staff, and events.

Jacksonville Newspaper Indexes

The Jacksonville Newspaper Indexes were created to supplement the local newspapers referencing news stories and vital statistics from The Jacksonville Journal, The Florida Times-Union, and The Florida Times-Union Star Edition.

Great Fire of 1901

This collection contains original hand-written and typed telegraphic correspondence, and photographs related to the Great Fire of 1901 in Jacksonville, Florida.

Postcard Collection

This collection holds postcards that were donated or purchased for Jacksonville Public Library. The originals are held in the Special Collections Department.

Stereograph Collection

This collection holds stereographs that were donated to the Jacksonville Public Library. The originals are held in the Special Collections Department.

Lewis Ansbacher Map Collection

The Lewis Ansbacher Collection contains maps and engravings donated to the Jacksonville Public Library through the generosity of the Lewis and Sybil Ansbacher Family Foundation; Sybil Ansbacher, and Jordan Ansbacher.

Jacksonville City Directories

This collection holds reproductions of the Jacksonville City Directories. The originals are held in the Special Collections Department of the Main Library.

Florida Collection Photographs and Slides

Photographs and slides in the Jacksonville Public Library Florida Collection.

Jacksonville Public Library Neighborhood Project

Jacksonville, the largest city in Florida, has changed so much over the years. Founded in 1822, it is important for Jacksonville to have an abundance of photographs to archive the city’s rich history from its founding to the present.

1960 City Hall Time Capsule, Jacksonville Florida

A time capsule was discovered in the cornerstone of the former City Hall on Bay Street when the Public Works crew conducted pre-demolition work on the building. The time capsule had been placed there in October 1960 by then-mayor Haydon Burns.

Jacksonville Public Library Rare Books Collection

The Digital Collection contains materials selected from the Jacksonville Public Library (FL) Special Collections Department. This material is in the public domain unless otherwise noted.

Jacksonville Public Library Board of Library Trustees

A collection of minutes from the monthly meetings of the Jacksonville Public Library Board of Library Trustees (BOLT).

Hamilton Jay Collection

A scrapbook of newspaper articles collected/written by Florida poet and editor of The Florida Times-Union from 1888-1899: Hamilton Jay.

Cross-Florida Barge Canal

This collection contains photographs taken during the initial construction of the Cross-Florida Barge Canal in 1935.

Jacksonville Historic Newspapers

This collection holds digitized copies of historic newspapers published in Northeast Florida.

Springfield Home Tour Booklets

This collection contains the home tour booklets for the Springfield neighborhood from 1979 to present, both the spring tour led by Springfield Preservation and Restoration and the holiday tour led by Springfield Improvement Association and Archives.

Imagination Squared

Imagination Squared is a joint art exhibition between the Museum of Contemporary Art and Jacksonville Public Library which showcases local artwork.

Jacksonville Yearbook Collection

The Jacksonville Yearbook collection features an extensive archive of Duval County high school yearbooks spanning from 1907 onwards. This digital compilation aims to include yearbooks from all high schools in Duval County, offering a comprehensive overview of the region's educational history. Explore the collection to uncover the stories, faces, and memories that have defined the high school experiences of generations in Jacksonville.
